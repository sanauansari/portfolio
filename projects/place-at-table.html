A Place at the Table — Book Cover Redesign

Print Typography Book Cover

A full book jacket redesign for the middle-grade novel by Saadia Faruqi and Laura Shovan, created for GDS 035, Graphic Design I. The minimalist design emphasizes themes of friendship, food, and cultural identity through a curated palette, cooking utensil motifs, and a spice platter pattern.

Role

Graphic Designer — full book jacket redesign including front cover, back cover, spine, and flaps.

Tools

Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Process

For the final version, based on feedback from the previous draft, I experimented with the colors of the front and back flaps, using the color palette from my inspiration board to find a harmonious scheme. I adjusted fonts and font sizes on the front cover to create clearer hierarchy and improve readability, and swapped the U.S. flag bunting for cooking utensils to emphasize the cooking club at the center of the story.

On the back cover, I resized the review text for better balance and added a spice platter pattern to both covers to add visual interest and tie into the book's content. To keep the flap text from washing out, I changed the font color to white for stronger contrast against the background, and rearranged visual elements on the front cover and flap.

For the front cover specifically, I chose a minimalist approach because I thought it would grab attention and spark enough curiosity for someone to pick up the book.
